# Do Prediction Markets Lead the Polls? A Time-Series Analysis of Polymarket and Traditional Polling Aggregates During High-Volatility Election Events

## What this article covers
The Lead-Lag Relationship between Prediction Markets and Traditional Polling Aggregates.

## Hypothesis question
Do Polymarket price signals systematically lead changes in traditional polling aggregates by roughly 48–72 hours during high-volatility election events?

## Research topics
- Lead-lag dynamics between market-implied probabilities and poll averages
- Information efficiency of prediction markets under political news shocks
- Granger-causality / cross-correlation methods for election forecasting time series
- Poll aggregation lag sources (fieldwork windows, reporting delays, smoothing methods)
- High-volatility event windows (debates, legal rulings, candidate withdrawals, macro shocks)
- Hybrid forecasting approaches combining markets and model/poll signals
